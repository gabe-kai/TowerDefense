## Locked V1 Training Factions

The current first-playable training trio is locked as:

- Sabine Merrow: `Civic Order`
- Ash-Hart of the Boundary: `Terrain Management`
- Aurelian Vale-Sun: `Rune Construction`

These three factions define the first core ways the player learns to think about settlement defense.

## Current Faction Roles

### Sabine Merrow

Training role:

- `Civic Order`

Core identity:

- civic legitimacy
- protective governance
- warning, shelter, and recovery

Core map logic:

- compact and readable settlement
- fair burden-sharing
- strong post-wave restoration

Main danger:

- over-caution and visible neglect

Signature military technique:

- brief broomstick sortie with grenades and medium-strength fireballs
- most collaborative communication style about use of the technique

### Ash-Hart of the Boundary

Training role:

- `Terrain Management`

Core identity:

- sacred geography
- beastkin frontier authority
- boundary rites
- terrain-sensitive placement

Core map logic:

- distributed anchors
- strategic asymmetry
- rerouting and respecting passage

Main danger:

- fragmented outer sites and misread land

Signature military technique:

- personally led herbivore stampede with high power and high friendly-risk potential
- least likely to warn the tower before acting

### Aurelian Vale-Sun

Training role:

- `Rune Construction`

Core identity:

- magical geometry
- Asteri ceremonial order
- recursive settlement patterns
- roads and districts as spell structure

Core map logic:

- align, compose, and preserve pattern integrity
- build runes within runes
- treat space as enchantment infrastructure

Main danger:

- cascading pattern collapse from local damage

Signature military technique:

- rune-network teleportation for precision repositioning and emergency extraction
- somewhat communicative, but usually on Aurelian's terms

### Orren Voss

Future role:

- `Construct Industry`

Core identity:

- factory districts
- golem labor
- mechs and war-frames
- machine uptime and repair

Core map logic:

- protect assembly and maintenance infrastructure
- replace vulnerable labor with built systems
- cluster high-value machine districts while managing catastrophic breach risk

Main danger:

- machine chain collapse, specialist loss, and social hollowing

## Strongest Current Contrasts

### Sabine versus Ash-Hart

This is:

- civic order versus territorial attunement
- compact settlement versus distributed anchors

### Sabine versus Aurelian

This is:

- practical stewardship versus symbolic composition
- roads as logistics versus roads as sigils

### Sabine versus Orren

This is:

- human social stability versus controlled mechanization
- protecting households versus replacing exposure with constructs

### Ash-Hart versus Aurelian

This is:

- land fit versus imposed geometry
- old paths versus ideal pattern

### Ash-Hart versus Orren

This is:

- living landscape versus industrial replacement
- negotiated boundary versus controlled manufactory

### Aurelian versus Orren

This is:

- magical pattern planning versus machine production planning
- ceremonial recursion versus industrial uptime

## What the Locked V1 Trio Adds

Sabine adds:

- the baseline human settlement loop

Ash-Hart adds:

- landscape identity as faction identity

Aurelian adds:

- magical settlement planning as a real build doctrine

Together, the locked V1 trio teaches:

- how to protect a settlement socially
- how to shape a settlement through terrain
- how to shape a settlement through constructed rune geometry

## What Orren Adds Later

Orren adds:

- construct labor as a faction identity
- machine defense as settlement doctrine
- a lower-population, higher-industrial answer to frontier survival

He should arrive when the project is ready to support:

- construct assignment
- machine repair and uptime
- clearer industrial district risk
- social consequences of replacing live labor
