# Orren Voss Master and Faction Design

Status: Active
Scope: Faction
Phase: Pre-production
Owner: Design
Source of Truth: Yes

This document defines Magister Orren Voss as a future master and faction for the tower defense + settlement builder game.

It is intentionally scoped beyond the first V1 training trio. The goal is to create a master whose strategy revolves around factories, constructed labor, and defenses staffed by golems, mechanical frames, and other built workers rather than relying primarily on live villagers.

## Role of This Master

Orren should be the faction for players who want to solve defense through fabrication, logistics, and machine deployment.

This faction should answer the question:

"What if the settlement survives by replacing vulnerable labor with built labor?"

Orren is no longer just the industrial supply-chain master.
He should be the master of:

- factory districts
- golem labor
- mech crews or piloted frames
- supply-fed machine defense
- minimizing the number of living bodies exposed to routine risk

## High Concept

The Constructive Alchemical Industrialist sees the settlement as a controlled production organism whose greatest weakness is the fragility and unpredictability of mortal labor.

In Orren's ideal frontier:

- dangerous work is mechanized
- repetitive work is automated
- heavy defense is built, maintained, and fed by factory systems
- living villagers are fewer, more protected, and increasingly supervisory rather than manual

This faction's core fantasy is:

- build a tower-centered factory settlement
- manufacture golems, work frames, and heavy defensive machines
- move from hand labor to constructed labor
- keep the war machine running through stock, fuel, and repair

## Master Identity

### Working Name

**Magister Orren Voss**

### Social Position

- human
- alchemical engineer and applied artificer
- estranged from metropolitan institutions
- practical authority rooted in capability rather than legitimacy

### Biography

Orren Voss made his reputation through applied magical industry rather than courtly advancement. He worked in workshops, proving yards, and experimental manufactories where every spell had to justify itself in output, stability, and scalable use. His break with the center came after disputes over method, safety, ownership of invention, and the acceptable replacement of human labor.

The frontier suits him because it strips away ceremony and reveals the only argument he trusts: whether a system works under stress. To Orren, peasants dying on roads, haulers collapsing under loads, and militia lines breaking from panic are not proof of noble tragedy. They are proof that too much of society still depends on fragile organic parts.

He does not dream of a bustling thriving village in the ordinary sense. He dreams of a controlled stronghold in which a small number of living specialists direct a much larger body of constructed workers and war-frames.

### Temperament

Orren is:

- austere
- methodical
- intensely practical
- emotionally cool
- fascinated by optimization
- impatient with wasteful sentiment

The key flaw is dehumanizing abstraction.

He can mistake reduced exposure for humane governance even when the social result is alienating, hierarchical, and spiritually sterile.

## Governing Philosophy

Orren believes survival belongs to the settlement that removes fragile labor from the most dangerous parts of existence.

That means:

- dangerous hauling should be mechanized
- trench, wall, and heavy-yard labor should be mechanized
- frontline defense should increasingly rely on built systems
- skilled living labor should supervise, maintain, command, and invent

He values:

- repeatability
- controllability
- uptime
- repairability
- reduced dependence on morale-sensitive labor pools

### Command Stance

Orren's commands should usually feel:

- `Pragmatic`
- `Severe`

He can occasionally sound protective, but in a machine-logic way:

- preserve trained operators
- keep apprentices out of breach zones
- withdraw villagers from tasks that should belong to frames

### What Orren Rewards

Orren responds well to:

- replacing exposed labor with constructs
- increasing machine uptime
- protecting factory and repair yards
- maintaining reserve parts and fuel
- assigning living workers to supervision instead of brute labor

### What Orren Dislikes

Orren reacts poorly to:

- needless hand hauling
- live labor in predictable kill-zones
- underused foundries or assembly yards
- improvised local heroics instead of durable machine solutions
- decorative projects with no structural or industrial function

## Why This Faction Functions Differently

This faction changes the player's planning question from:

"How do I make the village function?"

to:

"How do I substitute built systems for vulnerable bodies?"

The player should care more than usual about:

- factory placement
- assembly throughput
- machine repair cycles
- fuel, mana, and component supply
- operator safety
- keeping core construct lines online under pressure

Damage matters differently here because destruction is not only loss of stock or route capacity.
It is loss of machine uptime.

## Relationship to the Bound Spirit

Orren treats the bound spirit as an executive intelligence for system control.

The relationship should feel:

- cool
- technical
- respect-based
- less social than Sabine's
- less mystical than Ash-Hart's
- less aspirational than Aurelian's

He values:

- measurable outcomes
- stable machine coordination
- quick rerouting after breakdown
- proof that a design can scale

The spirit is not his conscience.
It is the control mind best suited to oversee a complex constructed estate.

## Relationship to Villagers, Retainers, and Laborers

### Villagers

Orren wants fewer villagers exposed to the frontier's default risk profile.

That does not mean he is warm toward them.
It means he prefers them:

- sheltered
- trained for specialist roles
- used as supervisors, operators, and maintainers where possible
- gradually displaced from repetitive danger work

### Retainers

Orren's retainers should include:

- master artificer
- yard foreman
- route clerk
- operator instructor

### Laborers

In Orren's faction, "laborer" increasingly becomes a transitional category. Live labor still exists, but the faction fantasy is to reduce dependence on it over time through constructed teams.

## Architecture and Settlement Flavor

### Architectural Direction

- brick kilns, soot-stained plaster, iron fittings, riveted frames
- assembly sheds, proving yards, gantries, hoists, cranes, rail-like haul tracks
- fuel stores, component racks, charging platforms, maintenance pits
- smoke, heat, sparks, and visible machine silhouettes

The mood should be:

- industrial
- disciplined
- heavy
- controlled
- unsettlingly inhuman

### Layout Preference

Orren favors:

- direct industrial roads
- yard-centered districts
- linked foundry and assembly zones
- protected machine depots
- repair-first logistics

The resulting settlement should feel like a defended manufactory with attached habitation, not a village that happens to have workshops.

## Tutorial Role

Orren should tutorialize future advanced play around:

- replacing labor with constructs
- machine uptime as a strategic metric
- repair and redeployment loops
- industrial vulnerability and concentration risk
- specialist living populations supporting built workforces

He should not be part of the first V1 training trio.

## Unique Bonuses

### 1. Construct Labor

Orren should gain access to built workers that can handle dangerous, repetitive, or heavy jobs better than ordinary villagers.

Possible units:

- haul golems
- digger frames
- builder automatons
- loader rigs

### 2. Factory Discipline

Assembly yards, repair bays, and machine depots should perform better when organized into coherent industrial chains.

### 3. War-Frame Defense

This faction should gain access to mech-like or heavy constructed defense platforms that become stronger when properly fueled, maintained, and supplied.

Possible examples:

- tower-linked sentry frames
- repeat-load bolt rigs
- furnace-powered breach walkers

### 4. Reduced Civilian Exposure

Orren should be better than most masters at keeping living workers out of repetitive hazard zones once his construct economy is functioning.

## Unique Pressures and Weaknesses

### 1. Catastrophic Machine Failure

When industrial chains break, the consequences should be immediate:

- stopped constructs
- idle war-frames
- stalled repairs
- dead depots

### 2. Extreme Concentration Risk

Construct and mech play naturally encourages dense high-value factory districts.

When those districts fall, losses should be dramatic.

### 3. Social Hollowing

Minimizing live labor exposure should come with social costs.

Possible expressions:

- lower belonging
- unease around replacement by constructs
- weaker village identity
- tension between operators and ordinary households

### 4. Specialist Bottlenecks

Even with many constructs, Orren still depends heavily on a smaller pool of skilled living workers for:

- maintenance
- assembly
- calibration
- command

This means losing the wrong few people can hurt him badly.

## Faction Rules

### Rule 1: Built Workforce

The faction can replace categories of ordinary labor with construct teams as infrastructure matures.

### Rule 2: Machine Uptime

Constructs and war-frames gain value only when fueled, repaired, and properly assigned.

### Rule 3: Protected Specialists

The faction works best when living workers shift upward into specialist roles while dangerous routine work is offloaded onto machines.

## Starting Settlement Package

Orren's opener should suggest an unfinished mechanized estate.

### Starting Buildings

- `Tower Core`
- `House`
- `Storehouse`
- `Workshop Yard`
- `Assembly Shed` or `Foundry Annex`
- 1 haul road spine
- 1 maintenance or loading platform

### Starting Personnel

- 1 founding household
- 1 master artificer
- 1 yard foreman
- 1 operator apprentice
- fewer general live laborers than comparable factions

### Starting Resource Bias

Orren should start with:

- better industrial potential
- earlier access to constructs
- lower baseline dependence on ordinary labor

## Starting Spell, Rune, and Policy Kit

## Starting Spells

### Assembly Impulse

Temporarily boosts construct assembly or repair speed at a chosen industrial site.

### Control Beacon

Improves coordination, retasking, or response speed for nearby constructs and machine defenses.

### Inventory Sight

Lets the player inspect the state of a factory node, depot, or machine team from the tower.

### Vitriol Lance

A practical offensive spell with industrial-alchemical flavor.

## Starting Runes

### Conveyor Sigil

Improves transfer speed across a marked industrial route.

### Command Lattice

Improves coordination and response for linked constructs in a local zone.

### Repair Seal

Improves recovery speed for damaged constructs, mechs, or machine yards.

## Starting Policies

### Replace the Dangerous Work

Prioritizes construct assignment to the riskiest repetitive jobs, reducing civilian exposure at increased industrial demand.

### Protected Specialists

Keeps apprentices, artificers, and operators out of frontline risk when possible, improving long-term stability but reducing emergency flexibility.

### Continuous Maintenance

Improves machine reliability and repair readiness at ongoing resource cost.

## Command and Quest Patterns During a Wave Cycle

### Recovery Phase Commands

Examples:

- restore the assembly line before dawn
- recover the damaged haul golems before rebuilding the outer sheds
- return the breach frame to service before the next omen matures

### Preparation Phase Commands

Examples:

- complete a second loader frame before the next major wave
- shift stone hauling to constructs and withdraw villagers from the cut
- stock enough fuel and parts for two frame activations

### Imminent Threat Commands

Examples:

- hold the sentry frames in reserve until the outer wall is tested
- preserve the maintenance pit even if the outer store is lost
- do not waste operators on hand labor while the breach walkers stand idle

### Minor Requests

Examples:

- test a new armature on the east yard
- assign a promising childless worker to operator training
- clear a jam in the south conveyor route

## Scope Control

Orren's future identity should focus on:

- a small set of construct labor categories
- one or two mech-like defense types
- clear machine uptime states
- strong contrast between built and living workforces

Avoid:

- huge unit rosters
- deep factory tycoon chains
- full RTS mech micro

## Implementation Priorities

If Orren becomes an active production target later, the most important Orren-specific elements are:

1. one clear construct labor system
2. one repair-and-uptime model
3. one or two iconic war-frame defenses
4. strong UI clarity around machine state and assignment
5. social consequences for replacing live labor

## Open Questions for Next Pass

- Which labor categories are best replaced first in the tech ladder?
- Should mech units be autonomous, crewed, or mixed?
- How visible should village unease around constructs be in normal play?
