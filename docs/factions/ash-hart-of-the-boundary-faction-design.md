# Ash-Hart of the Boundary Master and Faction Design

Status: Active
Scope: Faction
Phase: Pre-production
Owner: Design
Source of Truth: Yes

This document defines Ash-Hart of the Boundary as a V1 master and faction for the tower defense + settlement builder game.

It is intentionally V1-focused. The goal is to create a master whose gameplay is meaningfully different from the civic human baseline by changing how the player reads land, places structures, and prepares for waves.

## Role of This Master

Ash-Hart should be the first strongly beastkin and landscape-driven master in the roster.

This faction should answer the question:

"How does a settlement survive when it treats the land as a partner, witness, and weapon?"

Ash-Hart should not feel like a reskinned human town. The faction should make players rethink:

- where buildings belong
- what counts as a defensive line
- how a settlement spreads
- which spaces are spiritually safe or dangerous

## High Concept

Ash-Hart of the Boundary is a land-bound druidic sovereign whose tower is less a civic office than a vertical marker of covenant between settlement and frontier.

Ash-Hart does not seek to impose civilized order in the imperial sense. Instead, Ash-Hart seeks to bind dangerous land into an intelligible border: not tame, not conquered, but named, warded, and persuaded into unstable coexistence.

This faction's core fantasy is:

- read the land like an omen text
- build around groves, ridges, springs, hollows, and crossings
- anchor safety in sacred paths, standing wards, and boundary rites
- turn terrain and mana geography into part of the defense network

## Master Identity

### Working Name

**Ash-Hart of the Boundary**

### Nature and Social Position

- beastkin
- ancient but still embodied in the world
- tied to thresholds, paths, and border places
- recognized as a local power by covenant rather than rank

### Biography

Ash-Hart is older than the current villages of the region and possibly older than the tower site itself. Before the tower was raised, boundary stones, herding ways, hunting paths, and taboo groves already marked this land as a place of passage and danger. Ash-Hart remembers those older divisions.

Whether Ash-Hart was once mortal should remain uncertain in V1. What matters is that Ash-Hart embodies the belief that settlements endure only when they acknowledge the hungers, routes, and moods of the land around them. The beastkin aspect should matter culturally and physically: Ash-Hart is not merely a human with antlers, but a frontier power whose body, instincts, and social memory are rooted in animal lineage as well as personhood.

Ash-Hart accepts that monster waves are not just armies to be defeated. They are migrations, disturbances, infestations, and spiritual weather. To survive them, the settlement must occupy the right places, leave the wrong places open, and teach danger where to pass.

### Temperament

Ash-Hart is:

- patient
- uncanny
- attentive
- territorial
- difficult to rush
- responsive to trespass and imbalance

The primary flaw is alien priority.

Ash-Hart can care intensely about a spring, grove, ridge, old shrine path, or beast corridor in ways that seem irrational to human survival logic.

## Governing Philosophy

Ash-Hart believes safety is created by right relation to the frontier.

That means:

- know where passage wants to happen
- do not build blindly across every path
- make the settlement porous in some places and closed in others
- honor living and magical boundaries so threats can be redirected rather than only resisted

Ash-Hart values:

- fit
- flow
- attunement
- distributed watchfulness
- protective taboo

### Command Stance

Ash-Hart's commands should usually feel:

- `Protective`
- `Pragmatic`
- occasionally `Severe` in a ritual or territorial sense

Orders should feel like warnings, injunctions, and rites of preparation rather than administration.

### What Ash-Hart Rewards

Ash-Hart responds well to:

- building in accord with terrain
- preserving sacred or sensitive nodes
- using distributed anchors instead of overconcentrating everything at the tower
- keeping beast paths, streambeds, and boundary spaces intelligible
- rerouting danger instead of always meeting it head-on

### What Ash-Hart Dislikes

Ash-Hart reacts poorly to:

- clear-cutting or flattening without restraint
- blocking movement corridors indiscriminately
- building over spiritually charged spaces carelessly
- treating the land as empty backdrop
- concentrated smoke, fire, or waste in living places

## Why This Faction Functions Differently

This faction is powerful when the player learns to:

- read terrain as strategy
- accept asymmetrical settlement growth
- protect multiple anchored sites
- let some areas remain open or semi-wild for strategic reasons

This changes the loop:

1. Survey for lines of passage, old boundaries, and mana mood.
2. Place structures where they harmonize with defensible terrain.
3. Establish sacred anchors and boundary wards before brute fortification.
4. Redirect or soften threats before they hit dense habitation.
5. Recover by restoring damaged relationships to place, not only buildings.

## Relationship to the Bound Spirit

Ash-Hart treats the bound spirit as a witnessing intelligence attached to the tower but not identical with it.

The relationship should feel:

- old
- ritualized
- watchful
- less hierarchical than Sabine's
- more initiatory than administrative

Ash-Hart values:

- patient observation
- listening to signs
- respecting forbidden or potent places
- choosing when not to intervene directly

## Relationship to Villagers, Retainers, and Laborers

### Villagers

Ash-Hart sees villagers as people who can belong to a place if they learn its terms.

Ash-Hart prefers:

- households tied to safe ground
- route-aware labor
- respect for taboo spaces
- coexistence with managed wild edges

### Retainers

Ash-Hart's retainers should feel like:

- ward-keepers
- path-watchers
- herb tenders
- beast lore keepers

### Laborers

Ash-Hart expects labor to be shaped by place. Work should feel less like "nearest efficient job" and more like "the right person in the right zone."

## Architecture and Settlement Flavor

### Architectural Direction

- timber, turf, stone, woven hurdles, carved posts, and standing stones
- shrines, markers, woven charms, and way-gates
- clustered homes on good ground with outlying sacred or practical satellites
- paths that bend with terrain rather than forcing straight civic grids
- visible thresholds, rings, and marked crossings

The mood should be:

- folkloric
- rooted
- weathered
- protective
- alive to the frontier

### Layout Preference

Ash-Hart favors:

- distributed placement
- terrain-aware clustering
- sanctified choke points
- sacred paths and protected crossings
- buildings that acknowledge contour, water, old groves, and visibility lines

## Tutorial Role

Ash-Hart should tutorialize the idea that the landscape itself is a gameplay system.

This faction should teach:

- why open ground and blocked ground are different decisions
- why asymmetry can be strategically correct
- why outlying sacred nodes matter
- why the player should sometimes preserve terrain features instead of flattening them
- why waves can be shaped before reaching the core settlement

Ash-Hart should not tutorialize complexity for its own sake.
The faction should still teach a legible doctrine:

- the map has strong places
- strong places want different kinds of use
- the wrong build can make a defensible site weaker rather than stronger

## Difficulty and Player Appeal

Recommended difficulty:

- medium

This faction should appeal most to players who enjoy:

- map-reading
- asymmetrical planning
- terrain-first strategy
- folkloric settlement identity

The difficulty comes less from punitive scarcity and more from learning a different idea of what a "good settlement" looks like.

## Initial AI Behavior Profile

If Ash-Hart is used as an AI-controlled faction on the shared map, the initial AI should skew toward:

- valuing terrain features and outer anchors earlier than other factions
- leaving some paths open on purpose
- building fewer dense urban cores
- investing in edge warning and shrine coherence before heavy wall saturation

The AI should look visibly different from Sabine even at a glance.

## Unique Bonuses

### 1. Boundary Attunement

Ash-Hart should gain stronger effects from building near recognized terrain and mana features when placement is correct.

Possible outputs:

- stronger local wards at springs, ridges, groves, and old stones
- reduced mana cost for place-bound effects
- improved omen clarity from correctly tended edge sites

### 2. Sacred Paths

Marked paths and crossings should provide more than movement.

Possible effects:

- safer evacuation routes
- improved runner or guard response between anchors
- more reliable fallback movement through rough terrain

### 3. Living Edge

Ash-Hart should be better at using partially wild edge territory without fully domesticating it.

Possible effects:

- stronger outer warning when fringe land remains intelligible
- less severe performance loss from distributed edge sites
- more resilient transitions between inhabited and half-wild zones

### 4. Soft Redirection

Ash-Hart should be better at influencing where danger goes than at simply enduring frontal impact.

Possible expressions:

- stronger path-biasing boundary magic
- chokepoint wards that turn, split, or slow pressure
- safer partial openness on deliberately managed routes

## Special Military Technique

Ash-Hart should have a direct battlefield intervention that feels primal, territorial, and dangerous even to use well.

### Boundary Stampede

Ash-Hart can personally lead a stampede of heavy herbivores through a chosen route or threatened zone.

What It Does:

- summons or drives a short-lived charge of massive herd beasts
- strongest when launched along readable terrain lines such as slopes, cuts, fords, or open lanes
- can shatter light enemies, disrupt formations, and force route reconsideration
- carries meaningful risk to friendlies, structures, livestock, or sacred sites if used badly

Power Profile:

- the land itself surging in anger
- a terrifying territorial answer rather than a tidy spell
- high power with high commitment
- rewards players who truly understand terrain and open-space management
- gives Ash-Hart a spectacular but not low-skill panic button
- reinforces that this faction does not solve problems the same way Sabine or Aurelian do

Risks:

- if too safe, it loses identity and becomes a generic summon
- if too uncontrollable, it becomes a trap choice instead of an advanced skill expression

Request and Autonomy:

- the player should be able to request that Ash-Hart unleash this technique
- Ash-Hart should also be willing to trigger it independently when territorial logic or immediate threat makes the answer obvious
- Ash-Hart should be the least communicative of the V1 trio about intent beforehand

AI Use Heuristics:

- Ash-Hart may not think to report back to the tower before acting
- if the land is being offended or a route must be violently cleared, Ash-Hart may simply move
- the spirit often learns of the decision through signs, motion, and impact rather than dialogue first
- Ash-Hart should prefer using the stampede only where terrain, route shape, and collateral risk make it feel like a territorial judgment rather than random aggression

## Unique Pressures and Weaknesses

### 1. Spatial Complexity

This faction asks the player to manage more anchored sites and more irregular placement than Sabine.

This should be one of Ash-Hart's biggest practical costs in V1.

### 2. Alien Priorities

Ash-Hart may ask the player to preserve places that seem expendable in purely human strategic terms.

This should create tension between:

- immediate pragmatic survival
- long-horizon territorial health

### 3. Lower Urban Efficiency

Ash-Hart should usually be worse than Sabine or Orren at tight civic logistics and concentrated throughput.

### 4. Fragmentation Risk

A distributed settlement is powerful when coordinated, but vulnerable if anchors are isolated or cut off.

This means Ash-Hart should reward players who understand route coherence, not just pretty wilderness theming.

## Faction Rules

### Rule 1: Named Boundaries

Certain terrain features and crossings can be marked, sanctified, or bound for stronger local effects.

### Rule 2: Right Placement

Buildings and wards gain more from correct land placement than from pure adjacency or density.

### Rule 3: Distributed Covenant

The faction is strongest when several meaningful sites are linked in a coherent boundary network rather than collapsed into one compact town.

## Unique Support Structures

Ash-Hart should likely gain at least one support structure distinct enough to carry faction identity even if the underlying building roster remains mostly shared.

### Boundary Shrine

A `Boundary Shrine` is a small sacred anchor placed at a path edge, crossing, grove entrance, ridge cut, or other threshold.

Functional direction:

- stabilizes local place-bound effects
- improves omen clarity from that edge
- supports safe retreat or rerouting along linked sacred paths
- serves as a meaningful outlying site the player wants to maintain without overbuilding

This should not become a second tower.
It is a minor but spiritually potent anchor.

The `Way-Gate` concept can remain as a future support structure or upgrade path, but the primary V1 signature structure should be the `Boundary Shrine`.

## Starting Settlement Package

### Starting Buildings

- `Tower Core`
- `House`
- 1 food site suited to local terrain, such as a `Pasture`, grove, or field edge
- 1 outlying `Boundary Shrine`
- 1 marked path connection between tower and anchor
- 1 modest store or household cache

### Starting Personnel

- 1 founding household
- 1 ward-keeper
- 1 path-watcher or runner
- 1 herb tender or beast-keeper

### Starting Resource Bias

Ash-Hart should start with:

- slightly stronger place-bound magic potential
- slightly weaker compact logistics than Sabine
- earlier access to distributed warning and boundary tools

The opener should communicate from the first few minutes that one important site is not next to the tower and that this is a feature, not a mistake.

## Starting Spell, Rune, and Policy Kit

## Starting Spells

### Boundary Sense

Reveals spiritually charged crossings, old path lines, or disturbed thresholds without fully scouting the surrounding terrain.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that not all strategic information is topographic or economic
- tells the player where the land itself is "loud"

### Tangle Ward

Creates temporary difficult ground or barrier effects that work best at chokepoints and edges.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that terrain-aware defense can be created as well as discovered

### Way Blessing

Improves movement, evacuation, or coordination along a marked sacred path.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that paths can be defensive systems

### Briar Lance

A direct offensive spell with naturalistic flavor, useful but not the faction's main strength.

Tutorial purpose:

- keeps the faction from feeling helpless without redefining it around direct damage

## Starting Runes

### Boundary Stone

Marks a local territorial edge and strengthens nearby place-bound effects.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that fixed anchors matter as much as buildings

### Way-Knot

Links two safe paths or anchor sites for improved travel or coherence.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches the player to think in connected territorial networks

### Watch Charm

Placed at an edge site, crossing, or grove to improve omen reliability and local warning.

Tutorial purpose:

- reinforces edge intelligence rather than tower-only intelligence

## Starting Policies

### Respect the Old Path

Improves movement and harmony on preserved routes, but discourages building directly over them.

Tutorial purpose:

- makes preservation itself an actionable strategic choice

### Keep the Living Edge

Improves outer-site resilience and warning if some wild fringe remains intact.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that not all unused land is wasted land

### Seasonal Labor

Encourages labor assignments that fit terrain and season, reducing risk at some throughput cost.

Tutorial purpose:

- reinforces that Ash-Hart is safer through fit, not sheer output

## Command and Quest Patterns During a Wave Cycle

### Recovery Phase Commands

Examples:

- re-raise the boundary stones on the south path
- do not leave the spring hollow fouled through nightfall
- restore the grove marker before the next omen deepens

### Preparation Phase Commands

Examples:

- open the ridge path and close the marsh crossing
- raise a ward at the east standing stone
- move the herd from the low ground before dusk

### Imminent Threat Commands

Examples:

- do not meet them in the birch hollow
- ring the inner path and leave the western cut open
- hold the ford only long enough to turn them uphill

### Minor Requests

Examples:

- spare a marked tree during clearing
- rebury disturbed remains near the old stones
- assign a calm watcher to the north path

These minor requests should help Ash-Hart feel territorial and memory-laden rather than bureaucratic.

## Tutorializing the Intended Game Loop

Ash-Hart's faction should deliberately teach the following:

### Lesson 1: Terrain Has Strategic Meaning

Use:

- `Boundary Sense`
- place-bound bonuses
- edge-aware warning tools

This teaches:

- a hill, grove, spring, ford, or ridge cut can matter before any wall is built

### Lesson 2: Distributed Sites Can Be Strong

Use:

- sacred anchors
- outlying warning sites
- path-linked retreat and coordination

This teaches:

- not every healthy settlement is compact

### Lesson 3: Partial Openness Can Be Safer Than Total Closure

Use:

- soft redirection
- deliberately managed routes
- choke-point wards

This teaches:

- defense is sometimes about teaching danger where to go

### Lesson 4: The Wrong Build Can Injure a Place

Use:

- weaker outcomes for insensitive placement
- stronger outcomes for right placement
- faction disapproval around spiritually offensive use

This teaches:

- construction is not value-neutral

### Lesson 5: Recovery Includes Restoring Boundaries

Use:

- repair of sacred anchors
- restoration of fouled or broken threshold sites
- priority on path and shrine coherence after damage

This teaches:

- aftermath is territorial as well as material

## How Ash-Hart Differs from Later Masters

Compared to Sabine:

- less civic
- less compact
- more willing to leave space semi-wild

Compared to Aurelian:

- values fit over ideal form
- treats irregularity as natural rather than flawed

Compared to standard fantasy-druid archetypes:

- Ash-Hart should feel specifically frontier, territorial, and settlement-facing
- the beastkin identity should matter to worldview and social shape, not just silhouette

Compared to Orren:

- values living edge over controlled manufactory
- distributes where Orren concentrates

Compared to later harsher or stranger masters:

- Ash-Hart should feel uncanny and demanding, but not sadistic
- the faction's difficulty should come from learning a new map logic, not from arbitrary punishment

## Scope Control

Ash-Hart's V1 identity should focus on:

- terrain-aware placement
- distributed anchor sites
- path and boundary logic
- limited rerouting and place-bound bonuses

Avoid in V1:

- full ecology simulation
- deep druidic life cycles
- too many unique terrain rules per biome

If necessary, scope should be protected by reducing the number of meaningful terrain feature categories rather than flattening the faction back into a normal settlement with green visual flavor.

## V1 Implementation Priorities

If implementation bandwidth is limited, the most important Ash-Hart-specific elements are:

1. one clear place-sensing tool
2. one strong boundary-anchor system
3. path-based movement and warning benefits
4. tangible reward for terrain-sensitive placement
5. clear visual language for sacred edges and linked sites

## Areas for Refinement

- Decide how visible "offended land" should be to the player in normal UI versus debug or development tooling.
- Decide whether Ash-Hart gets one truly unique food or pastoral building, or relies on placement logic layered onto shared buildings.
- Decide how physically bestial Ash-Hart's supporting beastkin culture should appear in housing, tools, and ritual spaces.

## Open Questions for Next Pass

- How many terrain feature categories should count for Ash-Hart in V1?
- How much can Ash-Hart actually reroute waves versus merely biasing or softening their approach?
- Should Ash-Hart use fully unique building visuals, or variants layered onto shared core structures?
