# Sabine Merrow Master and Faction Design

Status: Active
Scope: Faction
Phase: Pre-production
Owner: Design
Source of Truth: Yes

This document defines Lady Sabine Merrow, the first playable master for the tower defense + settlement builder game.

It is intentionally V1-focused. The goal is to establish a clear baseline faction that teaches the intended game loop, aligns with the existing economy and social systems, and provides a stable reference point for later harsher or stranger masters.

## Role of the First Playable Master

The first playable master should be the game's easy-mode baseline.

This does not mean trivial.

It means the master's instincts, incentives, and unique tools should usually reinforce the player's healthiest first-playable habits:

- protect households
- preserve labor
- build clear logistics
- invest in warning and preparedness
- favor sustainable recovery over reckless gambles
- use the tower's authority in ways that feel fair and legible

This master should teach the intended loop before future masters begin subverting it.

## High Concept

The first playable master is a human frontier magistrate-mage: a lesser noble sent to bring order, continuity, and lawful prosperity to a dangerous edge territory.

She is a real mage, but not primarily a duelist, occultist, or tower-recluse. Her training and ambition are rooted in administration, mediation, records, public works, and the responsible use of magical authority.

Her faction fantasy is:

- hold the line against chaos
- build a civilized foothold on a dangerous frontier
- make the tower feel like a seat of shelter and order
- govern in a way that earns obedience instead of burning through it

## Master Identity

### Working Name

**Lady Sabine Merrow**

The name is provisional, but the role should lean hard into the frontier magistrate fantasy rather than generic "good noble wizard."

### Social Position

- human
- low nobility
- educated in law, administration, rhetoric, and formal magic
- appointed to the frontier as a tower-holder, magistrate, and civil authority

She should feel close enough to ordinary people to understand their burdens, but still undeniably part of the ruling class.

### Biography

Sabine Merrow was raised in a minor but respected noble house whose fortunes depended less on courtly brilliance than on dependable service. Her family supplied clerks, magistrates, quartermasters, negotiators, and provincial wardens to the core empire. They were not powerful enough to escape consequences when administration failed, and not distant enough from their tenants to ignore famine, raids, or winter death.

As a young woman, Sabine served in road offices, granary courts, and regional survey commissions before earning formal magical distinction. Her spellcraft developed alongside bureaucratic duty: warding archives, tracing land boundaries, stabilizing storehouses, reading ley disturbances, and maintaining signaling works. She built a reputation as someone sent where things had gone wrong and needed to become governable again.

Her current posting is both burden and opportunity. The frontier is dangerous, provincial, and politically neglected, but it is also a place where she can prove that the tower can embody the empire at its best rather than merely extracting obedience from the vulnerable.

### Temperament

Sabine is:

- disciplined
- observant
- courteous without being soft
- socially responsible
- restrained in crisis
- uncomfortable with waste, spectacle, and panic

Her key flaw is over-caution.

She is the kind of ruler who would rather secure a perimeter, audit supplies, and prepare three fallback plans than gamble on a risky strike. This makes her reliable in emergencies, but it also creates the danger of slow wars, tolerated nests, and problems that grow worse because they were managed instead of decisively crushed.

## Governing Philosophy

Sabine believes the tower is not merely a private residence or magical instrument. It is an office.

Its purpose is to:

- impose order on danger
- shelter dependents
- coordinate labor
- preserve memory and law
- make survival less arbitrary for the people under its shadow

She does not think common people are equals in authority, but she does think they are owed competent stewardship.

This gives her a strongly matriarchal political style. She sees the settlement as an extended household under noble care: structured, unequal, and disciplined, but not disposable.

### Command Stance

Her orders should skew toward:

- `Protective`
- `Pragmatic`

She should only rarely issue:

- `Severe`
- `Sacrificial`

When she does escalate, it should feel reluctant and consequential.

### What She Rewards

Sabine responds well to:

- shelter restored quickly after damage
- fair and visible sharing of hardship
- stable food and housing
- sensible roads and storage
- clear warning coverage
- protection of children, households, and skilled workers
- disciplined rebuilding

### What She Dislikes

Sabine reacts poorly to:

- avoidable civilian deaths
- elite safety paired with worker exposure
- hoarded supplies during obvious hardship
- repeated panic drafting
- chaotic layouts that impede evacuation and hauling
- wasteful magical showmanship
- neglected repairs

## Why She Functions as Easy Mode

Sabine is easy mode because she rarely pressures the player away from sound first-playable decisions.

Her faction identity supports the intended baseline loop:

1. Survey from the tower.
2. Secure food, labor, and storage.
3. Improve warning and shelter.
4. Prepare defensive lines and response routes.
5. Survive the wave with minimal social collapse.
6. Restore housing, morale, and logistics quickly.

She helps players learn that:

- social legitimacy is a practical resource
- civilians matter because labor and households matter
- warning time is powerful
- compact orderly layouts outperform sprawling improvisation
- recovery between waves is as important as the wave itself

Her strengths are strongest when the player behaves competently and responsibly, which makes her an ideal teaching master.

## Relationship to the Bound Spirit

Sabine treats the player, the bound spirit, as the lawful intelligence of the tower rather than as a servant to be shouted at.

She sees the spirit as:

- the eye of the tower
- its memory
- its watchfulness made active
- an officeholder with duties rather than a pet or chained familiar

This relationship should feel formal, cooperative, and slightly ceremonial.

She explains why orders matter.
She expects accountability.
She respects competent judgment.

If the player ignores her repeatedly, she should become colder and more official, not erratic or abusive.

This makes her a good first master because her command language teaches systems clearly instead of obscuring them behind vanity, cruelty, or occult logic.

## Relationship to Villagers, Retainers, and Laborers

Sabine distinguishes between roles and rank, but she does not treat common people as expendable.

### Villagers

She sees villagers as subjects whose safety, discipline, and productive continuity are part of the tower's duty.

She prefers:

- households kept intact where possible
- fair burdens during crisis
- visible shelter policy
- dependable food and storage
- measured drafting only when danger is plain

### Retainers

She values retainers as extensions of office and order.

Her retainers should include practical civil roles such as:

- steward-clerk
- tower servant
- lookout or guard-sergeant

These characters help communicate faction identity away from pure military fantasy.

### Laborers

She respects labor functionally and morally.

In her view, builders, haulers, field workers, and quarry hands are not romanticized, but neither are they disposable. Skilled labor loss should feel especially painful under her faction because it represents both human cost and institutional failure.

## Architecture and Settlement Flavor

Sabine's settlement should look like a civilized frontier outpost built by people who expect to endure.

### Architectural Direction

- stone tower base with timber or plaster upper works
- whitewashed walls and dark roofs
- banners, bells, notice boards, and boundary markers
- practical courtyards and service lanes
- compact housing close enough for warning, shelter, and repair
- modest heraldic decoration with civic meaning rather than vanity excess

The mood should be:

- orderly
- provincial
- dignified
- defensible
- inhabited

This should read less as an eccentric wizard compound and more as a small seat of lawful authority on dangerous land.

### Layout Preference

Sabine favors:

- compact footprints
- clear roads
- visible gates
- layered fallback lines
- protected storage
- defensible work sites

Her faction aesthetics should reinforce readability from the tower-centered perspective.

## Tutorial Role

Sabine should tutorialize the intended first-playable loop instead of fighting it.

Her faction should teach:

- how warning stages matter
- why villagers must be sheltered before impact
- why roads and storage improve survival
- why builder capacity is critical after damage
- why compact layouts and chokepoints are easier to defend
- why legitimacy erodes when the tower appears unfair
- why recovery priorities matter immediately after a wave

She should not tutorialize exotic systems first.

She is the faction that says:
"Learn how this world works when the tower is trying to be responsible."

## Difficulty and Player Appeal

Recommended difficulty:

- easy

This faction should appeal most to players who enjoy:

- clear priorities
- protective stewardship
- compact settlement planning
- socially legible choices

Sabine should be the easiest faction to read correctly even when she is not always right.

## Initial AI Behavior Profile

If Sabine is used as an AI-controlled faction on the shared map, the initial AI should skew toward:

- compact, orderly growth around the tower
- early food, storage, and warning investment
- visible concern for shelter and repair
- fallback-minded defensive planning rather than reckless aggression

The AI should read as competent, humane, and somewhat cautious from the first few wave cycles.

## Unique Bonuses

Sabine's bonuses should center on social stability, fair authority, and orderly recovery rather than raw military aggression.

### 1. Civic Legitimacy

Protective and pragmatic orders generate less resentment and recover trust faster after compliance.

Implications:

- villagers tolerate emergency discipline better if the tower is visibly protecting homes and dependents
- the player gets a wider safety margin while learning command timing
- legitimacy is still fragile, but less punishing than with harsher masters

### 2. Matriarchal Stewardship

Households recover morale and belonging faster when:

- they are rehoused quickly
- food stores are reopened after a wave
- children and sole caregivers are visibly protected

This bonus should lean hard into social stability rather than population growth or free resources.

### 3. Ordered Recovery

Repair, hauling, and reconstruction targeting should be easier to keep efficient under Sabine.

This can be expressed through:

- better default rebuild priorities
- slightly improved builder response to high-priority structures
- stronger post-wave recovery speed for housing, gates, storage, and mana infrastructure

### 4. Administrative Warding

Basic warning, shelter, and coordination magic should be more dependable under her than offensive spellcasting.

She should feel magically competent without becoming the best offensive caster.

## Special Military Technique

Sabine should have a limited personal battle intervention that feels brave, practical, and slightly old-fashioned rather than overwhelming.

### Magistrate's Sortie

Sabine can take to the air on a broomstick for a brief combat sortie.

What It Does:

- short-duration flight over local battlefield space
- a few carried grenades for clustered or high-value targets
- a few medium-strength fireball casts from the air
- limited charges or uses per wave cycle

Power Profile:

- a dignified but real willingness to share danger
- an emergency stabilizer rather than a battle-winning superweapon
- visible proof that Sabine is a real mage, not only an administrator
- strong enough to rescue a failing gate, clear a breach cluster, or support a fallback
- weak enough that the faction still wins through readiness, shelter, and recovery rather than relying on Sabine as a hero unit

Risks:

- if too strong, it undermines the faction's civic-order identity
- if too weak, it feels like flavor instead of a meaningful intervention

Request and Autonomy:

- the player should be able to request that Sabine use this technique
- Sabine should also be willing to initiate it on her own when she judges a sector to be in real danger
- she should usually communicate her intent before launching or be open to quick negotiation if time allows

AI Use Heuristics:

- Sabine wants to work with the bound spirit as a team
- she is the most likely of the three V1 masters to explain her plan, accept input, and treat the spirit as a battlefield partner rather than merely an observer
- she should prefer using the sortie to stabilize breaches, cover orderly retreat, or protect critical civic structures rather than chase kills

## Unique Pressures and Weaknesses

Sabine's faction should still carry real pressure.

### 1. Duty of Care

When obvious dependents die through neglect, the fallout should be stronger under her than under more callous masters.

Especially sensitive cases:

- children caught exposed without cause
- sole caregivers lost to reckless orders
- sheltered households destroyed because basic repairs were ignored

### 2. Public Fairness Standard

If the tower appears to preserve elite or magical assets while laborers take the risk, legitimacy drops harder.

This teaches an important faction lesson:
fairness is not abstract morality, it is visible political reality.

### 3. Over-Caution

Sabine is excellent at surviving pressure, but weaker at decisive eradication.

Her faction should be somewhat worse at:

- fast aggressive suppression
- reckless expeditions
- high-risk offensive magic
- "solve it now at any cost" play

This creates a signature danger:
if the player is too passive, threats can consolidate and produce drawn-out attritional pressure.

This should be a real gameplay bias rather than pure flavor.

Recommended expression:

- Sabine more often favors fallback, containment, reserve posture, and infrastructure protection over aggressive pursuit
- some wave-cycle commands should intentionally under-commit to threat eradication
- if the player disobeys at the right moment and achieves a clean success, Sabine should be capable of revising her judgment and granting increased respect or favor

This creates healthy tension:
the easy-mode master teaches sound fundamentals, but still leaves room for player mastery through selective, intelligent disobedience.

### 4. Lower Tolerance for Ugly Success

Some other masters will accept victory bought through terror, burn rates, or expendability.
Sabine should not.

This makes socially costly emergency solutions still available, but more politically expensive.

## Faction Rules

The following V1 faction rules are recommended.

### Rule 1: Office of Protection

The tower gains better social outcomes when orders visibly prioritize shelter, essential supplies, and recovery.

Practical hooks:

- lower legitimacy loss from evacuation or temporary labor reassignment when tied to visible danger
- better follow-through on post-wave repair priorities

### Rule 2: Shared Burden

Drafting, rationing, or emergency labor feels more acceptable when burden is seen as distributed fairly.

Practical hooks:

- reduced loyalty penalty if retainers and trained defenders are also exposed
- increased loyalty penalty if noncombatants bear costs while tower staff remain insulated

### Rule 3: Cautious Doctrine

Fallbacks, reserves, and layered defense are more effective than reckless forward commitments.

Practical hooks:

- better performance for evacuation and fallback orders
- weaker reward profile for risky offensive doctrines

## Starting Settlement Package

Sabine's opener should be slightly more orderly and protected than a neutral baseline, without skipping the core build-out game.

### Starting Buildings

- `Tower Core`
- `House`
- `Field`
- `Woodcutter Camp`
- a partially completed civic `Storehouse` foundation as an early guided objective
- a small connected road spine between tower, house, field, and stockpile
- a unique `Bell Post`

### Starting Personnel

- 1 founding household of 4
- 1 steward-clerk retainer
- 1 tower servant
- 1 lookout or guard-sergeant

This keeps the starting social picture legible while reinforcing her civil-administrative identity.

### Starting Resource Bias

Sabine should not begin with the strongest mana economy.

Instead, her package should lean slightly toward:

- better initial storage and logistics
- better protected food path
- one small readability or warning advantage

The partially completed `Storehouse` should serve as a guided opening objective that teaches:

- why protected storage matters
- why builders and hauling are part of survival
- why recovery infrastructure is not optional flavor

## Starting Spell, Rune, and Policy Kit

Sabine's starting kit should be low complexity, broadly useful, and strongly tutorializing.

## Starting Spells

### Survey Light

Revised direction:

`Survey Light` should not reveal terrain, passability, or hidden map layout.

Instead, it briefly reveals resource signatures on the strategic map without revealing the map around them. The player can detect that something valuable exists in a distant direction, but must still send real scouts or workers to learn the terrain and route.

This preserves the importance of exploration while giving Sabine a responsible, administrative magic identity.

Most likely reveal targets:

- ley line traces
- mana well signatures
- hidden mana crystal deposits
- major stone deposits
- rich timber stands

It should feel like magical surveying, not clairvoyant scouting.

### Household Ward

A low-complexity defensive spell that temporarily protects a house, storehouse, gate, or similarly important civic structure.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches the value of protecting infrastructure instead of only killing enemies
- helps first-time players triage key assets during a bad wave

### Rally Chime

A local coordination spell that improves:

- evacuation response
- sheltering speed
- lookout alert transmission
- guard rally efficiency

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that command and response timing matter
- reinforces the tower as a coordination center

### Measured Bolt

A simple direct attack spell that gives the player some active magical intervention without redefining the faction around damage.

Tutorial purpose:

- lets the player feel like a real mage-servant pairing exists
- keeps offensive play present but secondary

## Starting Runes

### Alarm Rune

Placed on roads, gates, or approaches to report nearby hostile movement sooner and more clearly.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches warning placement
- reinforces route prediction and readiness

### Waymark Rune

Marks a route for better travel, hauling, or evacuation reliability.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that roads and movement planning are defensive systems

### Sentry Seal

Improves the quality or lead time of reports from a lookout structure or watch point.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that not all information comes from direct player vision

## Unique Support Structure: Bell Post

Sabine's faction should begin with a unique `Bell Post` rather than a generic lookout variant.

The `Bell Post` is a civic warning structure that emphasizes readiness and coordinated response rather than direct surveillance power.

Functional direction:

- improves readiness-task assignment speed when a warning stage advances
- accelerates local transitions into evacuation, sheltering, and guard muster
- improves reliability of "drop current task and respond" behavior for nearby workers and retainers
- provides a clearer settlement-level signal for fallback or lock-the-gate behavior

The `Bell Post` should not replace scouting, tower vision, or proper warning networks.

Its role is to make the settlement more legible and responsive once danger is known.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that readiness is not just information, but organized response
- gives Sabine a distinctive civic object that expresses her magistrate identity
- reinforces the difference between warning, readiness, and direct player sight

## Starting Policies

### Open Granary

Spend food to stabilize morale and belonging after a hard wave.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that stockpiles can be spent politically, not just materially

### Protected Households

Children and essential caregivers shelter earlier during warning stages, reducing social catastrophe risk at some labor cost.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches the civilian exposure model early

### Repair First

Prioritize restoration of:

- housing
- gates
- storehouse
- mana infrastructure

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches healthy post-wave recovery sequencing

### Ordered Levies

Improves emergency draft compliance when danger is obvious, but repeated use still accumulates resentment.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that coercion is viable but not free

## Command and Quest Patterns During a Wave Cycle

Sabine should be the clearest example of the intended "one major command and one minor request per wave cycle" cadence.

Her requests should be understandable, actionable, and tied to concrete civic logic.

### Recovery Phase Commands

Examples:

- rehouse all displaced households before nightfall
- reopen the main storehouse
- repair the east gate before the next omen matures
- bury the dead and restore order in the damaged quarter

These teach that aftermath is gameplay, not downtime.

### Preparation Phase Commands

Examples:

- extend warning coverage along the north road
- add a second food source before the next full wave
- establish a safer route between field and gate
- assign reserve shelter for exposed laborers

These teach that wave preparation is largely civic and logistical.

### Imminent Threat Commands

Examples:

- shelter all households beyond the outer ditch
- hold the main gate only until the bell sounds twice, then fall back
- protect the storehouse and mana well above outlying sheds
- do not draft sole caregivers unless the inner line is breached

These teach:

- fallback planning
- triage under pressure
- command stance readability

### Minor Requests

Examples:

- employ a displaced worker in a safer role
- restore a boundary shrine or civic banner
- inspect an unusual mana disturbance
- reassign a grieving retainer temporarily

These make her feel personal and administrative without bloating scope.

## Tutorializing the Intended Game Loop

Sabine's faction should deliberately introduce the following lessons through bonuses, commands, and starting tools.

### Lesson 1: The Tower Is an Information Hub

Use:

- lookout support
- alarm tools
- survey magic that reveals resources but not routes

This teaches:

- information is incomplete
- planning matters before contact
- scouting remains necessary

### Lesson 2: Civilians Are Core Gameplay

Use:

- social stability bonuses
- sheltered household expectations
- strong penalties for visible neglect

This teaches:

- villages are not background dressing
- losses have household and political consequences

### Lesson 3: Layout Is Defense

Use:

- roads
- compact settlement preference
- evacuation and fallback support

This teaches:

- defense starts in village planning

### Lesson 4: Recovery Wins Long Games

Use:

- repair priority support
- morale recovery through policy
- stronger rebuild rhythm

This teaches:

- surviving one wave badly can lose the next one

### Lesson 5: Fairness Has Mechanical Value

Use:

- legitimacy bonuses for protective commands
- legitimacy penalties for exposed labor inequality

This teaches:

- authority depends on how risk is distributed

### Lesson 6: Guidance Does Not Mean Obedience Is Always Correct

Use:

- Sabine's over-cautious command bias
- moments where aggressive success can outperform prudent delay
- stronger approval when the player disobeys a timid order and clearly succeeds

This teaches:

- the master is a personality, not an infallible AI advisor
- the player can earn respect through judgment, not just compliance
- V1 masters can differ meaningfully in how often their caution or ambition should be challenged

## How Sabine Differs from Later Masters

Sabine should be the faction that aligns with the intended core loop.

Later masters should diverge by asking the player to violate, distort, or reinterpret it.

### Compared to a Harsher Master

A harsher master may:

- tolerate ugly casualties
- demand stricter discipline
- push heavier drafting
- reward military efficiency over belonging

Sabine instead prioritizes stable legitimacy and household continuity.

### Compared to a Vain or Prestige-Driven Master

A vain master may:

- demand ornament during crisis
- prioritize tower grandeur
- treat civic needs as secondary to magical status

Sabine instead sees beauty as heraldic order and public meaning, not indulgence.

### Compared to a Stranger or Nonhuman Master

A stranger master may:

- require unusual geometry
- disregard human rhythms of labor and family
- use magic or architecture that rewrites baseline expectations

Sabine instead preserves a human-legible settlement pattern that teaches the shared baseline before that baseline is broken.

### Compared to a Ruthless Arcane Specialist

A ruthless specialist may:

- convert suffering into power
- prefer magical throughput over social cohesion
- accept deep long-term damage for short-term tactical gain

Sabine instead teaches that durable survival is built from order, memory, and care.

## Scope Control

Sabine's identity should not expand into a full law, tax, or class administration simulation in V1.

Keep the faction centered on:

- social stability
- fair authority
- warning and preparedness
- recovery quality
- compact orderly settlement planning

Avoid introducing:

- complex legal proceedings
- tax collection systems
- deep noble household politics
- broad diplomacy mechanics unique to her

Those themes can remain in flavor text, commands, and later expansion hooks.

## V1 Implementation Priorities

If implementation bandwidth is limited, the most important Sabine-specific elements are:

1. protective-pragmatic command tone
2. social stability bias
3. `Survey Light` as resource-signature revelation only
4. better post-wave recovery priorities
5. stronger punishment for visible neglect of dependents
6. frontier magistrate architecture and presentation

## Open Questions for Next Pass

The current direction is:

- Sabine begins with a partially completed `Storehouse` as a guided opening objective
- Sabine uses a unique `Bell Post` with readiness and response bonuses
- fairness and legitimacy logic should stay mostly diegetic in the main UI, with a debug-facing panel exposing the underlying numbers when needed
- Sabine's over-caution should be a real faction bias that creates opportunities for successful player disobedience

Open follow-up questions:

- How often should Sabine be wrong in a way that invites aggressive correction without making her feel incompetent?
- Should the debug-facing social panel be globally available in development builds, or unlocked through a dedicated debug view only?
- Should the `Bell Post` remain unique to Sabine, or should later factions inherit weaker or altered warning-bell equivalents?
