# Aurelian Vale-Sun Master and Faction Design

Status: Active
Scope: Faction
Phase: Pre-production
Owner: Design
Source of Truth: Yes

This document defines Aurelian Vale-Sun as a V1 master and faction for the tower defense + settlement builder game.

It is intentionally V1-focused. The goal is to create a master whose strategy revolves around magical geometry, recursive settlement patterns, and roads and districts used as components of a living rune.

## Role of This Master

Aurelian should be the V1 faction that most visibly turns the settlement itself into magical machinery.

This faction should answer the question:

"What if the village is not merely defended by spells, but arranged to become one?"

## High Concept

The High Enchanter of Splendor believes architecture, ornament, geometry, and magical power are all aspects of the same truth.

To Aurelian, a village is not merely a cluster of practical buildings. It is a ritual diagram, a visible statement of authority, and a machine for shaping mana through proportion, alignment, and recursive symbolic order.

This faction's core fantasy is:

- build a living rune across the landscape
- arrange roads, plazas, walls, and districts into magical patterns
- create power through alignment instead of only extraction
- preserve recursive structure under pressure

## Master Identity

### Working Name

**Aurelian Vale-Sun**

### Social Position

- Asteri
- high-born
- classically educated enchanter of the imperial heartland
- invested in ritual architecture and symbolic sovereignty

### Biography

Aurelian was formed by Asteri courts, observatories, academies, and palace-wards where architecture and ritual were inseparable from authority. He was trained to believe that a realm becomes governable when power is made visible in line, proportion, heraldry, procession, and repeatable magical form.

Where Sabine was educated to administer troubled places and Ash-Hart emerged from troubled land itself, Aurelian was educated to compose order so convincingly that it becomes reality. The frontier fascinates him because it is unfinished. It offers a chance to write an ideal pattern onto rough land and watch a true arcane settlement unfold.

He does not think beauty is indulgence. He thinks beauty is evidence of correct arrangement. As an Asteri, his people should carry associations with long study, celestial orientation, luminous craft, and a belief that truth is often revealed through relation, recurrence, and pattern.

### Temperament

Aurelian is:

- brilliant
- exacting
- charismatic
- ceremonially minded
- aesthetically sincere
- impatient with ugliness and compromise

The key flaw is abstraction.

He is capable of solving the wrong problem elegantly. He can ask the player to preserve symbolic continuity or complete a district plan when a more grounded master would simply demand food, walls, or fast repairs.

## Governing Philosophy

Aurelian believes true rule is expressed through visible pattern.

That means:

- architecture should instruct movement as well as shelter it
- ornament should reinforce magical and political cohesion
- roads should be lines of force, not only lines of travel
- districts should be composed, not merely accumulated

He is a formalizer.

### Command Stance

Aurelian's commands should usually feel:

- `Pragmatic`
- `Protective`
- sometimes `Prestige`-inflected even when practical

He should occasionally edge toward abstraction-driven demands that create friction with immediate survival logic.

### What Aurelian Rewards

Aurelian responds well to:

- completed alignments
- coherent districts
- roads that serve both traffic and sigil logic
- elegant repair that restores pattern, not just function
- ornament with structural or ritual meaning

### What Aurelian Dislikes

Aurelian reacts poorly to:

- asymmetrical patchwork
- districts left visibly broken
- emergency additions that never become integrated
- crude sprawl
- magical infrastructure treated as an afterthought

## Why This Faction Functions Differently

This faction changes the player's planning question from:

"Where should the buildings fit?"

to:

"What pattern should the whole settlement become?"

The player is optimizing for:

- symbolic alignment
- rune continuity
- recursive and nested structure
- preservation of key geometric relationships

This makes damage matter differently. A minor breach can break a larger magical circuit or district pattern.

## Relationship to the Bound Spirit

Aurelian treats the bound spirit as a co-architect of magical order.

Compared to Sabine's civic partnership, this relationship should feel elevated and exacting. He assumes the spirit can see latent harmonies and help realize them.

He praises:

- elegant execution
- beautiful resilience
- improvisation that preserves higher order

He dislikes:

- clumsy repairs
- brute solutions that save the moment while ruining the design
- neglect of symbolic infrastructure

## Relationship to Villagers, Retainers, and Laborers

### Villagers

He prefers a populace that visibly participates in coherent social order.

He values:

- ceremonial identity
- district dignity
- visible belonging
- settlements that look governed rather than improvised

### Retainers

Aurelian's retainers should include:

- chamberlain
- glyph attendant
- heraldic steward
- ritual surveyor

### Laborers

Aurelian respects skilled labor as the hand that makes ideal form real. His blind spot is not contempt for workers, but the assumption that proper form ultimately serves everyone.

## Architecture and Settlement Flavor

### Architectural Direction

- white stone, glazed tile, bright banners, gilt trim, patterned paving
- gate arches, processional routes, mirrored facades, nested courts
- luminous sigil inlays and decorated boundary markers
- plazas and districts that imply mathematical intention

The mood should be:

- radiant
- composed
- prestigious
- mathematically beautiful
- slightly unreal in its perfection

### Layout Preference

Aurelian favors:

- rings
- axes
- mirrored gate lines
- nested courts
- district patterns that echo larger settlement shapes

The village should feel like runes within runes.

## Tutorial Role

Aurelian should tutorialize advanced spatial planning.

This faction should teach:

- roads as magical infrastructure
- settlement layout as a source of arcane power
- the value of preserving designed relationships between structures
- how indirect damage can break systems
- how rebuilding can be about restoring pattern, not just replacing loss

Aurelian should not feel like "the prestige faction" in a shallow sense.
He should teach that ornament, roads, district shape, and magical function can all be part of the same system.

## Difficulty and Player Appeal

Recommended difficulty:

- medium to medium-hard

This faction should appeal most to players who enjoy:

- deliberate layout
- symbolic planning
- magical infrastructure
- highly authored settlement aesthetics

The challenge should come from maintaining coherent pattern under pressure, not from obscure hidden rules.

## Initial AI Behavior Profile

If Aurelian is used as an AI-controlled faction on the shared map, the initial AI should skew toward:

- building visible axes, courts, and aligned routes
- preserving district centers even when patchwork repairs would be faster
- favoring pattern-complete neighborhoods over pure opportunistic sprawl
- visibly differing from Sabine through ceremonial order and from Ash-Hart through imposed geometry

The AI should read as intentional and architectonic even before the player understands the full pattern system.

## Unique Bonuses

### 1. Pattern Resonance

Completed or nearly completed settlement patterns provide meaningful bonuses.

Possible outputs:

- lower mana upkeep on connected runes
- stronger ward radii
- increased spell stability
- stronger effects at aligned nodes

### 2. Sigiled Roads

Roads that participate in valid patterns should gain extra magical value.

Possible effects:

- enhance rune transmission
- improve guard and runner response on aligned routes
- strengthen nearby wards and district links

### 3. Splendor and Allegiance

Visible order and symbolic completion should support morale, loyalty, and faction cohesion when the settlement remains impressive and intact.

This should not become a generic beauty score.
It should reward visible composure, district dignity, and ritual completion.

### 4. Enchantment Infrastructure

Aurelian should be better at sustained prepared magic than at brute direct damage.

## Special Military Technique

Aurelian should have a battlefield intervention that feels elegant, tactical, and inseparable from his rune-network identity.

### Court Translation

Aurelian can teleport himself, and anyone he is physically touching, between valid points in his connected rune-network.

What It Does:

- requires connected and active rune infrastructure
- moves Aurelian instantly between prepared network nodes
- can carry a small number of physically contacted companions during translation
- enables rapid rescue, repositioning, and selective elite intervention

Power Profile:

- supreme command of prepared magical space
- a precise and aristocratic answer to battlefield crisis
- a technique that rewards good network construction before the wave ever begins
- makes Aurelian slightly more useful in active combat because he can appear exactly where needed
- makes him extremely important in emergency evacuation or extraction scenarios
- reinforces that his faction's true mobility is designed, not improvised

Risks:

- if too permissive, it trivializes positioning and evacuation
- if too restrictive, it stops feeling like a signature master technique

Request and Autonomy:

- the player should be able to request that Aurelian use this technique
- Aurelian should also initiate it on his own when preserving a critical district, person, or pattern-center clearly calls for it
- he should usually inform the spirit if doing so is elegant, convenient, or politically useful, but not always if he considers the need self-evident

AI Use Heuristics:

- Aurelian assumes the spirit ought to understand good pattern logic without needing exhaustive explanation
- he is more communicative than Ash-Hart, but less collaborative than Sabine
- his notifications should often feel like pronouncements of action rather than invitations to deliberate
- he should prefer using translation for emergency extraction, elite repositioning, and defense of high-value rune centers rather than for routine mobility

## Unique Pressures and Weaknesses

### 1. Fragility of Pattern

Reliance on grand alignment means one damaged district can create cascading weakness.

### 2. Slow Improvisation

Temporary fixes should be less satisfying here.

Possible expressions:

- temporary structures do not contribute fully to pattern bonuses
- emergency repairs restore function faster than resonance
- ugly asymmetrical additions create temporary friction until integrated

### 3. Prestige Pressure

Aurelian should sometimes demand symbolic completion or preservation in ways that pull against pure survival logic.

This is a real point of faction tension, not just a character quirk.

### 4. Abstraction Risk

He can ask the player to solve the wrong problem elegantly.

## Faction Rules

### Rule 1: Grand Pattern

The settlement can form valid magical patterns from roads, structures, anchors, and selected decorative elements.

### Rule 2: District Resonance

Clusters and alignments of properly related structures gain stronger local magical identity than isolated pieces.

### Rule 3: Recursive Order

The faction is strongest when local patterns echo larger ones, creating runes within runes.

## Unique Support Structures

Aurelian should likely have at least one distinctive support structure that makes the faction legible before the full pattern system is even understood.

### Rune Court

A `Rune Court` is a ceremonial district node that helps define a local pattern center.

Functional direction:

- anchors district resonance
- strengthens nearby aligned roads and structures
- acts as a visible "this part of the village is intentional" object

The `Crest Lantern` should remain an important rune object and route marker, but the primary V1 signature support structure should be the `Rune Court`.

## Starting Settlement Package

### Starting Buildings

- `Tower Core`
- `House`
- `Field` or court-garden equivalent
- 1 partially established `Sigil Road` spine
- 1 `Rune Court`
- 1 basic storage structure
- 1 decorative boundary marker set

### Starting Personnel

- 1 founding household
- 1 chamberlain or arcane steward
- 1 glyph attendant
- 1 ritual surveyor or grounds retainer

### Starting Resource Bias

Aurelian should start with:

- slightly better magical infrastructure
- less practical flexibility than Sabine
- stronger long-term scaling if pattern play is executed well

The opener should suggest an unfinished greater design the player wants to complete rather than a merely incomplete practical build order.

## Starting Spell, Rune, and Policy Kit

## Starting Spells

### Line of Splendor

Marks a road or structural line so it temporarily counts as a stronger pattern stroke.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that lines matter as magical objects
- gives the player a way to patch near-complete pattern logic during emergencies

### Harmonic Ward

Strengthens a structure or district more effectively if it is part of a valid pattern.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches conditional scaling from good layout

### Mirror Glance

Temporarily reveals the state of a connected rune node or aligned district from afar.

Tutorial purpose:

- supports distributed pattern monitoring from the tower
- reinforces that the player is managing a networked magical city-form

### Lustral Bolt

A respectable but secondary direct attack spell with ceremonial feel.

Tutorial purpose:

- keeps the faction from feeling non-interactive in combat without changing its identity

## Starting Runes

### Axis Seal

Placed on a centerline or district anchor to strengthen aligned features.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches the importance of centers, axes, and composition

### Circuit Stone

Connects one pattern node to another across a designed route.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that separation and linkage can both be deliberate

### Crest Lantern

Provides local morale, visibility, and symbolic completion when installed properly.

Tutorial purpose:

- lets decorative-symbolic placement feel mechanically meaningful from the start

## Starting Policies

### Ceremonial Streets

Improves benefits from roads that participate in valid patterns, but increases pressure to maintain them.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches roads as ritual infrastructure rather than pure hauling lanes

### Noble Facade

Improves cohesion from visibly complete districts at increased repair expectation.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches that visible order can be politically active

### Arcane Zoning

Encourages clustering of compatible structures for stronger local enchantment payoff.

Tutorial purpose:

- teaches district thinking instead of isolated building thinking

## Command and Quest Patterns During a Wave Cycle

### Recovery Phase Commands

Examples:

- restore the western axis before dusk
- do not leave the inner court visibly broken through the night
- re-establish continuity between the tower and south sigil road

### Preparation Phase Commands

Examples:

- complete the outer ring before the next serious wave
- align the east gate district with the main plaza
- establish a second anchor court north of the tower

### Imminent Threat Commands

Examples:

- preserve the central rune court at all costs
- hold the mirrored gates long enough for the circuit to complete
- if one district must be abandoned, let it be the unaligned quarter

### Minor Requests

Examples:

- install a crest lantern on the north processional road
- finish a district marker before sunset
- replace crude fencing with proper boundary stones

These minor requests should make Aurelian feel exacting and architectonic rather than merely vain.

## Tutorializing the Intended Game Loop

Aurelian's faction should deliberately teach the following:

### Lesson 1: Space Can Be Spellwork

Use:

- `Grand Pattern`
- `Line of Splendor`
- district anchors and linked runes

This teaches:

- the settlement itself can be the magical machine

### Lesson 2: Roads Can Carry Meaning Beyond Movement

Use:

- `Sigiled Roads`
- `Ceremonial Streets`
- axis and processional route logic

This teaches:

- route planning is magical planning

### Lesson 3: Local Damage Can Cause Systemic Failure

Use:

- broken district resonance
- severed pattern links
- lost symbolic centers

This teaches:

- one breach can harm an invisible larger design

### Lesson 4: Rebuilding Is Also Recomposition

Use:

- stronger rewards for integrated restoration
- weaker value from endless patchwork
- pressure to return a district to coherent form

This teaches:

- post-wave recovery is about more than replacing hit points

### Lesson 5: Order Can Inspire as Well as Empower

Use:

- `Splendor and Allegiance`
- visible completion
- crest and district identity

This teaches:

- aesthetics can have social and magical consequence when tied to doctrine

## How Aurelian Differs from Later Masters

Compared to Sabine:

- values symbolic composition over civic duty
- tolerates practical inconvenience more readily if higher form is preserved

Compared to Ash-Hart:

- imposes pattern where Ash-Hart negotiates with place
- sees irregularity as a problem to solve rather than a truth to honor

Compared to Orren:

- values composition over throughput
- treats roads as sigils before treating them as arteries

Compared to standard star-wise fantasy archetypes:

- Aurelian should feel courtly, luminous, and mathematical rather than elven or angelic by default
- the Asteri identity should matter through observatories, celestial motifs, long-pattern thinking, and luminous civic craft

Compared to later harsher or stranger masters:

- Aurelian should be demanding, but not merely selfish
- his faction should feel aspirational, exacting, and occasionally impractical, not sadistic or arbitrary

## Scope Control

Aurelian's V1 identity should focus on:

- a small set of pattern families
- reuse of normal buildings as pattern contributors
- clear visual feedback for valid and broken patterns

Avoid in V1:

- freeform rune drawing
- too many pattern templates
- precision requirements that feel fussy or brittle

If scope pressure rises, the right cut is to reduce the number of valid pattern families and district interactions, not to flatten the faction back into a normal settlement with a decorative buff layer.

## V1 Implementation Priorities

If implementation bandwidth is limited, the most important Aurelian-specific elements are:

1. one readable pattern-detection system
2. road-as-rune support
3. at least one strong district resonance payoff
4. good feedback when patterns are broken or restored
5. clear visual identity for ceremonial geometry

## Areas for Refinement

- Decide how strict pattern detection should be before it stops feeling expressive and starts feeling fussy.
- Decide whether some pattern families should be master-specific unlocks rather than all available immediately.
- Decide how strongly Asteri culture should differ architecturally from elite human ceremonial architecture in ordinary buildings, not just magical sites.

## Open Questions for Next Pass

- Which 3-5 pattern families are strong enough for V1?
- How explicit should the UI be in showing incomplete versus complete patterns?
- How much decorative placement is required before the faction truly feels distinct?
